My Editing Services

Proofreading

Correcting typos, misspellings, punctuation, grammar, and sentence structure.

Developmental Editing

One-three page detailed critique on overall meaning conveyed, emotional impact, voice/tone etc. with suggestions on how to improve. Proofreading is included.

Line-by-Line Editing

Line by line editing on word choice, line breaks, stanzas, imagery, syntax, length, etc. We will share our edited version of your poem as well as detailed line by line feedback explaining our changes and any suggestions or feedback. Proofreading is included. Rewrite, optional.

Poetry Book Comprising

Sorting through and helping you choose poems for your book/organizing them by theme & chapter theme suggestions. As well as optional format, layout, and basic design of your manuscript for self-publishing. Doesn’t include editing. Must upgrade.

My Editing Process

When doing a developmental edit or a line by line edit I always ask the client to share a brief background of what inspired the poem and what it is about/ means to them as well as details on what they personally feel needs to be improved upon so I can do my best to edit the poem in a way that will benefit them the most. It’s very important to have a clear idea of what they are looking for, so if needed I ask questions too. My end goal is always to make sure the client is happy with my work rather than perfection because ultimately they came to me for a reason and that reason should be fulfilled.

For a Developmental Edit

I separate the poem into stanzas and base my notes on each stanza. For poems that are not separated into stanzas, I divide the poem in the most sensible way, sometimes using colors to organize based on theme. I always do a light proofread first, fixing typos, misspellings, and grammar mistakes. The goal of a developmental edit it so share a detailed critique of the poem, specifically based on what the clients main concern with the poem is and feedback on how to improve rather than structure of the poem and line endings/line sequence. I never touch the client's poem unless absolutely necessary for an example, I only provide suggestions.

For a Line by Line Edit

I separate the poem into stanzas and each line. I get as detailed as possible, taking notes on every word and line. I give the most detailed feedback possible on what can be improved, my interpretation of meaning, themes, tone etc. I always do a light proofread first, fixing typos, misspellings, and grammar mistakes but as I edit each line I do a deeper dive fixing sequence of the lines and line adjusting endings. When the client agree to a rewrite I only add in my own words or lines if absolutely necessary and I always recommend they use my word or Semtex as an example and replace it with something personal to them. I always include my edited version of the poem to base my editing notes on.

For Proofreading

For a client who only requests proofreading of their poem or manuscript I do not do any deep editing (line by line or developmental) other than basic understanding of the poem. Proofreading is purely structural to fix line sequencing and line endings as well as fixing grammar, typo’s and any misspellings. I only provide a detailed explanation of my adjustments if the client requests it or if their are an abundance of mistakes.

For Poetry Book Comprising

When doing poetry book comprising (without editing) I always include a light proofread for typos. The main purpose is to help my clients with layout so their manuscript is ready for publishing as an ebook or paperback. As well as layout and formatting I help organize the poems, sort through which ones to include and which ones to exclude, I find themes so I can help find the best flow for the poems to tell a story through your words and suggest chapter themes. I offer basic designs and can assist with format and back covers if my client has a design in mind as I do not yet offer creation of designs/art.
