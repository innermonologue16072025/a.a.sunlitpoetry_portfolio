About Me

Hello, I'm A.A. Sunlit Poetry

I'm a 25-year-old writer from California specializing in editing poetry - whether it's a single poem or an entire manuscript. I help poets refine their work for personal projects or prepare for self-publishing.

My goal is to make your dream a reality and ensure you feel confident in your words. With seven years of reading and writing poetry, I've developed a deep appreciation for the craft and a passion for helping others perfect their poetic expressions.

I approach each project with sensitivity to your voice and vision, providing thoughtful feedback while preserving the emotional core of your work.

Poetry is when an emotion has found its thought and the thought has found words.
- Robert Frost

My Editing Services

Proofreading

Correcting typos, misspellings, punctuation, grammar, and sentence structure.

  • Basic structural corrections
  • Grammar and punctuation
  • No deep editing

Developmental Editing

Detailed critique on overall meaning, emotional impact, voice, tone, etc. with suggestions for improvement.

  • 1-3 page detailed critique
  • Focus on themes and emotional impact
  • Proofreading included

Line-by-Line Editing

Detailed editing on word choice, line breaks, stanzas, imagery, syntax, and length.

  • Detailed line-by-line feedback
  • Edited version provided
  • Proofreading included
  • Optional rewrite

Poetry Book Comprising

Helping choose and organize poems for your book with theme suggestions.

  • Poem selection and organization
  • Theme and chapter suggestions
  • Basic manuscript formatting
  • Doesn't include editing

My Editing Process

1

Understanding Your Vision

I begin by learning what inspired your poem and what it means to you. Your goals and concerns guide my approach.

2

Initial Read-Through

I read your work to understand its emotional core and overall flow before making any suggestions.

3

Detailed Analysis

Depending on the service, I provide stanza-by-stanza critiques or line-by-line feedback with suggestions.

4

Collaborative Refinement

I share my edits and feedback, and we work together to refine your poem until you're completely satisfied.

A Note on Death and Grief

Original Excerpt:

Death at least has the kindness of finality.

In one fell swoop, he wounds you with his scythe;
Always clean, always intentional.
Serving as proof that he was there.
But never does he try to cause more pain than necessary.

Edited Excerpt:

Death has the kindness of finality.

In one fell swoop, he wounds you with his scythe;
Always clean, always intentional.
Serving as proof that he was there.
Never does he try to cause more pain than necessary.

Feedback:

Removed "at least" to strengthen the opening line. Removed "but" from the last line to maintain the poem's powerful tone. Simplified phrasing for better flow while preserving the original meaning.

Back to You

Line-by-Line Editing for @thepoeticmoods â€¢ 05.22.25

Original Excerpt:

My heart is connected
with yours.
So even when my lips
stay pursed;
not mumbling your name.

Edited Excerpt:

Back to you

My heart has grown weary
of this constant tug of war.
Still, my heart is connected to yours.

My lips remain pursed,
never daring to mumble your name.

Feedback:

Added opening lines to establish the emotional conflict. Strengthened phrasing ("remain pursed" instead of "stay pursed"). Created a more poetic rhythm while preserving the core sentiment of connection despite silence.
